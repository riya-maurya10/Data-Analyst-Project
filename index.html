Scene 01 — Overview
The Big Picture
Our dataset covers 21,609 residential properties across Washington State. The average home sells for $511,619, with a combined basement area exceeding 38.6 million sq ft. This dashboard tells the story of what drives house prices — renovation history, age, and physical characteristics.

Scene 02 — Pricing
Price Distribution
The majority of homes fall in the $250K–$400K range with over 6,000 listings. The most populated bin is $300K with 2,188 homes. As prices rise, volume drops — only 1,042 homes exceeded $600K. Years-since-renovation stays low (0–1 years) across all price bands.

Renovation Patterns
Renovation rates increase sharply with house age. Homes aged 0–30 years show near-zero renovation rates, while homes aged 60–100 years have renovation rates of 10–20%. The 60–69 year age group shows the highest renovation activity — 159 homes renovated.

House Features by Age

Scene 05 — Insights
Key Takeaways
1. Price Volume: Most activity in $250K–$400K range; market thins above $500K.

2. Renovation Value: Older homes (60+ years) renovated at higher rates — investment potential in aged properties.

3. Modern Builds Dominate: 4-year-old homes are the single most common age — recent construction boom.

4. Stable Features: Bedroom/bathroom counts consistent across age groups — buyer preferences drive construction.
